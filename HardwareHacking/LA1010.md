# 🔍 Innomaker LA1010 USB Logic Analyzer

## 📖 Description
The Innomaker LA1010 is a high-performance USB logic analyzer with up to 100MHz sampling rate and 16 channels. It is an essential tool for reverse-engineering proprietary protocols, debugging communications, and sniffing traffic between microcontrollers.

## 🚀 Step-by-Step Example: Setup, Connect, and Use
**Scenario: Sniffing SPI traffic between a microcontroller and a radio module.**

### 1. Setup
* **Software:** Download and install the official KingstVIS software for your operating system. (It comes with the necessary proprietary drivers built-in).

### 2. Connect
* **USB:** Plug the LA1010 into your PC via the USB cable.
* **Wiring to Target:** Connect the provided test clips to the SPI traces on your target board.
  * LA1010 `GND` ➡️ Target `GND`
  * LA1010 `CH0` ➡️ Target `CS` (Chip Select)
  * LA1010 `CH1` ➡️ Target `CLK` (Clock)
  * LA1010 `CH2` ➡️ Target `MOSI`
  * LA1010 `CH3` ➡️ Target `MISO`

### 3. Use
* Open KingstVIS software.
* In the channel settings on the left, turn on `CH0` through `CH3` and turn off the rest to declutter the screen.
* Set the **Sample Rate** at the top. Rule of thumb: set it to at least 4x the speed of the target clock (e.g., 50MHz). Set depth to `10M` samples.
* Click the green **Start/Run** button in the top left, then immediately trigger an action on the target device (like pressing a button or powering it on).
* Stop the capture. You will see square digital waveforms.
* Click the **Analyzer** button (usually on the right side panel) and add the **SPI** protocol. Assign your specific channels to CS, CLK, MOSI, and MISO. The software will instantly decode the square waves into Hex/ASCII values!
